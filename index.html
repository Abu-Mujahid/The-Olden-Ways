The Olden Ways

Individualism, Mind Nurture, Nomadic

The Olden Ways is a quiet path back to foundations—where faith, fitrah, and time‑tested practice guide daily life. Curated by Umm Muḥammad al‑Banjābiyyah—a Muslim woman grounded in naturopathy and holistic living—this channel explores womanhood in Islam, resilient homes, and tradition‑rooted solutions for modern dilemmas. Each section gathers studies, notes, and reflections intended to inform, provoke thought, and invite responsibility for one’s own learning and choices.

Intent and Approach

This space is organized by topic so you can enter through the doorway you need today—whether affliction and remedies, reflections on individualism, home‑centered birthing, polygyny and marriage, or just worldly matters that touch on the everyday. Posts may include direct evidences (dalīl), sourced compilations, or curated materials for exposure and insight. Read with care, verify, and exercise your ʿaql—your reason—responsibly.

“I share stuff does not mean that I am upon and endorse all that is there or them. Some may be dalīl (evidence) and some is compiled research for exposure/insight purposes. You have been given some ʿAql, you are responsible for your own self. I’m at service to be reached out for anything I’m able.”
— Umm Muḥammad al‑Banjābiyyah
